Field and Lab Work

Field Work

During my PhD at the University of Birmingham (UK) and my first postdoctoral position at Eawag (Swiss Federal Institute of Aquatic Science and Technology), I investigated how anthropogenic pressures, nutrient perturbations, and temperature changes affect freshwater ecosystems. These projects, which combined laboratory and large-scale outdoor experiments, provided me with strong skills in experimental design, ecological data collection, statistical analysis, and the coordination of multidisciplinary research teams.

Since 2021, as a postdoctoral researcher in the international PONDERFUL and TRANSPONDER projects, I have led extensive fieldwork campaigns across Europe, coordinating personnel, logistics, and data management. My research focuses on pond biodiversity and ecosystem functioning, particularly processes related to greenhouse gas emissions, carbon sequestration, metabolism, and decomposition. These roles have further strengthened my expertise in field coordination, ecosystem monitoring, and the assessment of ecological functions, as well as my ability to manage complex international collaborations.

Lab Work

During my six months visit at the laboratory of Biology of Marine Molluscs and Ecosystem Associated at the University of Caen I acquired experience on animalâ€™s dissections, microtome-paraffin section, probe design, western blot, PCR, RNA extractions and in situ hybridization.

I start my PhD by learning how to work with Daphnia resting eggs (epphipia). Once I extracted the epphipia from dated sediment, I decapsulated them (Fig. 1) under the microscope, to then expose the egg to normal condition of light, temperature and nutrientes. This process increases the success of hatching eggs.

Then, during my PhD project I oversaw protocol optimisation, DNA and RNA extraction and quantification with several molecular biology techniques (Qubit, agarose gel, qPCR, tape station and nanodrop) to optimize the sample throughput required for population genomics and functional genomics investigations including automation and miniaturization of DNA and RNA extractions, amplifications, quality assurance, and sequencing, resulting in my ability to process 500 samples simultaneously. I also performed microsatellite analysis. During my postdoc, I performed DNA extraction and protocol optimisation for de novo sequencing (Fig. 2).

In addition to the molecular lab skills, I have developed strong expertise in freshwater zooplankton taxonomy. I perform species-level identification of key groups, including cladocerans, copepods, and rotifers. This work requires detailed morphological knowledge and precision techniques, which often involve microscopic dissection, bleach digestion to isolate trophi (jaws) of certain rotifer species.
